# Financial-Performance-Analysis-FY2013-2014

A full financial analysis and interactive Excel dashboard built on 700 transaction records spanning two fiscal years, covering five countries, five customer segments, and six product lines.

Financial analysis — Profit margin calculation, cost breakdown, segment and geographic performance analysis

CRISP-DM methodology — Applied a structured analytical framework from problem definition through to insight communication



What Was Built


Product Analysis sheet — Pivot Table comparing Net Sales, COGS, and Profit across all six product lines, with Paseo identified as the highest-revenue but also highest-cost product
Country Performance sheet — Revenue and margin breakdown across five markets (USA, Canada, France, Germany, Mexico), revealing a 4.2 percentage point margin gap between Germany (best) and the USA (lowest)
Segment Profitability sheet — COGS vs. Sales comparison across five customer segments, flagging Enterprise as the only loss-making segment at –$614,546
Monthly Trend sheet — Sales trend from Jan 2013 to Dec 2014, capturing the October peak ($21.7M) and March trough ($5.6M)
Discount Analysis — Breakdown of $9.2M in discounts given against gross sales, mapped to segment-level impact
Interactive Dashboard — Consolidated visual summary with Slicers enabling filtering by Country, Segment, Product, and Year
Written Report — Full analysis document with embedded charts, supporting data tables, and six prioritised recommendations for cost and margin improvement

Key Insights


The business is profitable but cost-pressured — $119.1M in net sales and $17.1M in profit across two years, but COGS sits at 85.6% of net sales, leaving little buffer for cost increases or further discounting
Enterprise is losing money — The only segment where COGS exceeds revenue, by approximately $614,546; pricing and product mix for this segment need immediate review
High revenue doesn't equal high profit — Paseo leads all products on revenue ($33M, 27.7% of total) but also carries the highest COGS of any product line, compressing its margin disproportionately
Channel Partners punches above its weight — The smallest segment by revenue, but the highest margin at approximately 68%, showing that volume is not a prerequisite for profitability
Germany outperforms the USA on margin despite similar revenue — Germany runs a 16.2% margin vs. the USA's 12.0% on a comparable revenue base, pointing to a cost structure problem in the US market rather than a revenue problem
Sales are highly seasonal — October alone accounts for 18.2% of annual revenue ($21.7M), while March is the weakest month at $5.6M — a near 4x swing with direct implications for cash flow, inventory, and staffing planning
