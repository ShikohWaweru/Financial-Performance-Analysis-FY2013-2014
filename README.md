## Financial Performance Analysis — FY2013–2014
Sales, Cost, and Profitability Review Across Products, Regions, and Customer Segments

A full financial analysis and interactive Excel dashboard built on 700 transaction records spanning two fiscal years, covering five countries, five customer segments, and six product lines.

### What Was Built

- **Product Analysis** — Pivot Table comparing Net Sales, COGS, and Profit across all six product lines, with Paseo identified as the highest-revenue but also highest-cost product at $28.2M COGS
- **Country Performance** — Revenue and margin breakdown across five markets, revealing a 4.2 percentage point gap between Germany (16.2%) and the USA (12.0%) on a comparable revenue base
- **Segment Profitability** — COGS vs. Sales comparison across five customer segments, flagging Enterprise as the only loss-making segment at –$614,546
- **Monthly Sales Trend** — Combined FY2013–2014 trend across 12 calendar months, capturing the October peak ($21.7M) and March trough ($5.6M)
- **Discount Analysis** — Breakdown of $9.2M in discounts given (7.7% of gross sales), mapped to segment-level impact
- **Interactive Dashboard** — Consolidated visual summary with Slicers enabling filtering by Country, Segment, Product, and Year
- **Written Report** — Full analysis document with four embedded charts, supporting data tables, and six prioritised recommendations organised across three action areas: pricing and cost, discounting and segment strategy, and planning and forecasting

### Key Insights

1. **The business is profitable but cost-pressured** — $119.1M in net sales and $17.1M in profit across two years, but COGS sits at 85.6% of net sales, leaving very little room to absorb further cost increases or heavier discounting
2. **Enterprise is the only loss-making segment** — COGS exceeds sales by $614,546; pricing, supplier terms, and product mix for this segment all need review before costs can sit below revenue again
3. **High revenue doesn't always mean high profit** — Paseo leads all products on revenue ($33M, 27.7% of total) but carries the highest COGS of any product line at $28.2M, compressing its margin disproportionately
4. **Channel Partners punches above its weight** — The smallest segment by revenue but the highest margin at approximately 68%, proving that a leaner cost base can deliver better returns than scale alone
5. **Germany outperforms the USA on margin despite similar revenue** — Germany: 16.2%, France: 15.5%, Canada: 14.3%, Mexico: 13.9%, USA: 12.0% — the USA's gap is a cost structure problem, not a revenue problem
6. **Sales are highly seasonal** — October alone accounts for 18.2% of annual revenue ($21.7M) while March is the weakest month at $5.6M — a near 4x swing with direct implications for cash flow, inventory, and staffing planning
7. **Discounting is concentrated where it hurts most** — $9.2M given in discounts overall, with evidence it is contributing to Enterprise's loss position in the lower-margin segments

### Recommendations Summary

| # | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Fix Enterprise's loss-making position — review pricing, supplier terms, and product mix | Sales & Procurement |
| 2 | Review Paseo's production costs — target a cost-reduction plan within one quarter | Operations |
| 3 | Use Germany's cost structure as a benchmark for the USA | Regional Finance |
| 4 | Tighten discounting, especially in Enterprise — implement a minimum margin threshold | Sales Leadership |
| 5 | Protect Government; grow Channel Partners from its high-margin base | Account Management |
| 6 | Build the October peak and March trough into budgets, inventory, and headcount plans | FP&A & Supply Chain |
